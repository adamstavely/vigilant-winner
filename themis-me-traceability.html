Axiomatic domain mapping
From mission intent to governed service, with a measure at every seam
THEMIS expressed in the Department of War applied Mission Engineering process. The mission decomposes into tasks, tasks map to the four accountability axes as capabilities, and capabilities map to the consolidated service set as systems. Provenance is realized under Origin; adversarial and alignment defense are realized under Competence. A bounded adequacy measure is defined at each domain, so any service traces upward to a mission objective and no service exists without a capability measure it satisfies.

MOP Measure of Performance
Σ γₖ (SPₖ / EPₖ) ÷ Σ γₖ  ·  performance vs. expected
⟐ The traceability claim MOP → MOC → MOE → MOS
Governance services
Services that realize an axis are justified by threshold rollup: a service's MOP rolls up to a capability MOC, to a task MOE, to the mission MOS. Falsification test: name the axis whose sufficiency (MOC) falls below its required level (RCS) when the service is removed. For every primary-realizing service, that axis can be named.
Platform substrate
Identity, gateway, gates, session, credential and tool planes are justified as enabling preconditions that every axis depends on, not as capability realizers. They sit beneath the measures hierarchy, not on it. Falsification test: name the axis that can be enforced at all without it.
Intelligence layer
The 15 intelligence-layer services compound analytical value over time rather than enforcing an accountability axis. They are justified against a separate mission-value objective, traced on its own measure, not the four-axis MOS. Falsification test: name the analytical value that stops compounding without it.
The four axes measure accountability, not total mission value. "Too many services" stays falsifiable, but the test differs by service class: a capability measure that falls below threshold, a precondition that cannot be removed without disabling an axis, or analytical value that ceases to compound.

Reading the threads. MT relates objectives to tasks · MCT relates tasks to the four accountability axes · MET relates capabilities to the consolidated service set. Provenance (MOIRAI) is realized under Origin; adversarial defense and alignment monitoring (Adversarial Service, JANUS) are realized under Competence, which here means reliable and safe performance including under attack and within the model's own agentic behavior. Adequacy scores are illustrative analytic scaffolds, bounded [0,1], not claims of linear mission relationships. The CALIBRATION measure inside c₂ is realized by a Bayesian posterior (TCS/MIMIR), not a linear ratio; the ME notation provides the traceability frame, not the internal computation.
